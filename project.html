Image Project/Program Description Status

The first project was a crowdfunded field trip for Verde Island's school children in October 2015. It was held at The Mind Museum where their home, the Verde Island Passage (VIP), was featured as the epicenter of marine biodiversity in the world. The goal was to supplement the children's knowledge, pride, and awareness of the importance of the VIP as the next generation who would hopefully take good care of it.

Reef Guide Program - AIDA 1 Training

From January 14 to 16m 2018 our 12 reef guide scholars took the AIDA 1, thanks to ManuMano Freedive Training Center's Mark Cruz and Tara Abrina. The course covered the basic theory of freediving, breathing and relaxation, proper form and techniques, and safety protocols such as buddying, towing, and rescuing.

Though we've funded their AIDA certification, we're still raising funds to further their education and training to become reef guides.

Completed

Reef Guide Program - Training Dive (February 10 to 11, 2018)

With the help of our friends from the UP Marine Biological Society and ISDA, the reef guide scholars were taught dive planning and management. Afterwards they simulated a tour where they brought participants around the island and to their best dive site, Pulong Bato.

Completed

Reef Guide Program - Graduation (March 10 to 11, 2018)

All the training and donations came together last March 10 to 11 for the reef guide scholars' graduation. For a weekend, our friends from ISDA, UP Marine Biological Society, and SCUBA Studio became tourists of Barangay San Andres, Isla Verde. The itinerary included banig-making, a community tour, a whole lot of eating, and a dive to cap it all off. Thank you to everyone who's contributed to the project so far in any way. This is only the beginning for Isla Verde and our reef guide scholars.
